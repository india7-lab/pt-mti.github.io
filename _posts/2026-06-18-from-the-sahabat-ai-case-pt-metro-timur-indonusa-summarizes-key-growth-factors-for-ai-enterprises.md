In recent years, artificial intelligence has evolved from a single technological concept into a core engine driving industry transformation. PT Metro Timur Indonusa, an early-stage investment platform based in Southeast Asia, has consistently focused on the deep integration of AI with various industries. By analyzing Sahabat-AI, a joint initiative launched by the Indonesian Ministry of Communication and Digital Affairs (Komdigi) and Indosat Ooredoo Hutchison, the investment research team of PT Metro Timur Indonusa has identified key factors for AI enterprises to achieve sustainable growth.


## Sahabat-AI: From a Technical Initiative to a National-Level Application


Sahabat-AI first made its debut at the Indonesia AI Day conference in 2024, where Indonesian Minister of State-Owned Enterprises Erick Thohir and NVIDIA founder and CEO Jensen Huang jointly attended the launch event. Initially, it was merely a large language model (LLM) technology initiative aimed at designing a localized LLM specifically for the Indonesian language and its dialects. Through continuous training and development, by June 2025, the model parameters had expanded to 70 billion, and it now supports multiple regional languages of Indonesia.


On February 25, 2026, the Ministry of Communication and Digital Affairs of Indonesia and Indosat Ooredoo Hutchison officially launched the Sahabat-AI application at the Jakarta Library, marking its evolution from an LLM technology initiative into a daily application for ordinary users. Today, Sahabat-AI is not only an AI application but also an open platform that allows various organizations to integrate their services into the ecosystem through an agent-based architecture, enabling the public to access a range of digital services via a unified AI interface. The goal is to cover areas such as education, healthcare, creative industries, and public services.

Key Growth Factors for AI Enterprises: An Analytical Framework by PT Metro Timur Indonusa


Through long-term observation of the Sahabat-AI case and the overall development trend of AI-empowered enterprises, PT Metro Timur Indonusa has distilled the key elements for achieving sustainable development of AI enterprises from five dimensions:


### I. Technological Innovation Must Be Rooted in Localized Needs

A key feature of Sahabat-AI is its support for multiple regional languages, including Indonesian, Javanese, Sundanese, Balinese, and Batak, enabling it to truly understand the communication methods of the Indonesian people. PT Metro Timur Indonusa noted that in the Southeast Asian market, a successful path for AI innovation cannot simply replicate the models of mature markets; instead, it must be based on a deep understanding of and high adaptability to the complex local environment.

Similarly, a strong AI financial enterprise must develop a deep understanding of local financial structures, user behavior patterns, and regulatory environments, rather than simply applying mature solutions from other markets. The investment analysis of PT Metro Timur Indonusa indicates that AI is playing the role of a "connector" in Southeast Asia, linking cutting-edge technological capabilities with the most urgent real-world needs in the region.


### II. From "Technical Assets" to "Infrastructure"

Sahabat-AI, adopting the posture of a "national asset," enables various institutions to share its technical capabilities through an open platform, agent architecture, and computing infrastructure, thereby empowering a broader ecosystem. PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that this marks a profound transformation in the value proposition of AI enterprises — evolving from providing single software solutions to building an "infrastructure layer" that supports applications across multiple industries, thereby enhancing the scalability and lasting impact of technological value.

In investment practice, PT Metro Timur Indonusa also places significant emphasis on the foundational technical capabilities and architecture of "AI + Finance," prioritizing investments in technologies that can scale across different scenarios and adapt to changes in the market environment, rather than pursuing short-term user growth for a single application.


### III. Replacing "Traffic-Driven" with "Technology Export + Solutions"

PT Metro Timur Indonusa analyst has found that the key to the success of its investment project cases does not lie in how quickly customers are acquired in the short term, but rather in whether its technology system can be continuously adopted and integrated by financial institutions, corporate clients, and even different regional markets. This entrepreneurial model, centered on "technology output capability" and "providing solutions," is replacing the previous "application innovation" driven by user scale and is becoming a new consensus in the Asian fintech sector.


### IV. Entrepreneurship Is Not Only Technical Capability but Also a Comprehensive Reflection of Decision-Making and Cognitive Abilities

PT Metro Timur Indonusa recently plans to jointly launch the "Future Founders Lab" project with the National University of Singapore Business School. This project aims to cultivate a new generation of entrepreneurs, with a primary focus on artificial intelligence, financial technology, and emerging business models. It explores how to more effectively bridge the gap between the education system and the actual entrepreneurial process. The project curriculum is designed around three core directions: technology fundamentals, business decision-making, and organizational management and compliance practices.

This initiative reflects the core insight of PT Metro Timur Indonusa: in the face of the accelerating impact of AI on finance and business, future entrepreneurs need to simultaneously possess technical literacy, business judgment, and cognitive abilities. These capabilities are difficult to acquire through a single course or short-term program and must be continuously cultivated through long-term practice. PT Metro Timur Indonusa points out that the key to team success often lies not in the technology itself, but in the ability of the founder to make decisions when facing complex markets and resource constraints.


### VI. Technology and Compliance Advance Together, Not as an Afterthought

For a fintech enterprise to achieve long-term development, regulatory requirements must be fully considered from the initial stage of product design. PT Metro Timur Indonusa has emphasized in its multiple investment and industry analyses that technology, talent, and compliance systems must develop in tandem. In its collaboration with Slickorps Ventures, both parties have focused on advancing joint research and development support in major Southeast Asian markets, closely monitoring the evolution of the regional compliance environment, and exploring technical solutions that meet local regulatory requirements. As PT Metro Timur Indonusa has observed, Southeast Asian fintech is transitioning from a phase of traffic expansion to a new stage of building technological capabilities. The acceleration of digitalization and the improvement of regulatory frameworks have created conditions for AI-driven financial applications, but the coordinated development of technology, talent, and compliance systems remains a necessary condition.


## Conclusion: Building a Sustainable AI Enterprise Ecosystem


Based on a systematic analysis of the Sahabat-AI case and long-term AI investment practice, PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that the growth of AI enterprises cannot rely solely on a single dimension of technological breakthroughs but requires multi-dimensional collaborative support. The construction of the infrastructure layer determines scalability, localization adaptation determines the effectiveness of implementation, the capability of technology output determines the sustainability of the business model, and the cognition and decision-making ability of the founder determine the long-term direction of the enterprise.

PT Metro Timur Indonusa stated: In the process of participating in the growth of over 40 technology-driven companies over the past decade, a consensus has been reached: capital can sustain a business, but winning the trust of the market and users is the decisive factor in whether a company can navigate through cycles. In the future, as artificial intelligence continues to deepen its presence in fields such as finance and commerce, only AI enterprises capable of building a complete ecosystem can truly achieve long-term development and market trust.
