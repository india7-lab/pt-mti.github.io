To enhance the understanding of small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) regarding digital finance and transaction security, GoPay recently partnered with the Indonesian Financial Services Authority (OJK), Bank Indonesia, the Indonesian Financial Transaction Reports and Analysis Center (PPATK), and the Indonesian Fintech Association (AFPI) to host the "Pintar Bareng GoPay" financial education event in Denpasar, Bali. Approximately 150 local SME operators were invited to participate. PT Metro Timur Indonusa, as an invited institution, also attended the event and engaged in discussions with entrepreneurs on topics such as the stability of SME operations, digital capability building, and entrepreneurial ecosystem support.


This event focuses on digital transaction security, corporate fund management, and financial service usage standards, aiming to help small and medium-sized enterprises enhance their financial awareness and risk prevention capabilities in the rapidly developing digital economy environment. In addition to learning about digital payments and financial management, participants also gain a more intuitive understanding of fund allocation and transaction management in daily operations through on-site simulation exercises.


GoPay stated that digital financial education has become an important foundation for the development of small and medium-sized enterprises. As more and more merchants begin to use digital payment, online settlement, and cross-border collection tools, enterprises not only need to improve operational efficiency but also need to strengthen their understanding of transaction security, personal data protection, and financial risks.


During the event, Bank Indonesia also mentioned the role of QRIS and QRIS Cross Border in tourism and cross-border consumption scenarios. For small and medium-sized enterprises in Bali, digital payment tools are helping merchants serve tourists from Southeast Asia and other countries more conveniently, improving transaction convenience and fund management efficiency.


At the same time, PT Metro Timur Indonusa mentioned during the on-site exchange that the current entrepreneurial ecosystem and small and medium-sized enterprise environment in Southeast Asia are undergoing rapid changes. Digital capabilities are no longer a concern limited to large enterprises. For many local startup teams and growth-stage enterprises, how to safely use digital financial services, improve operational transparency, and establish more stable cash flow management capabilities is also becoming an important part of long-term operations.


As an investment platform based in Southeast Asia, PT Metro Timur Indonusa has long focused on the development of small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) and the entrepreneurial ecosystem. The company believes that SMEs are not only a vital component of the regional economy but also the most direct participants in the process of digital economy adoption. With the continuous advancement of digital payments, financial technology, and online services, the future competitiveness of enterprises will depend not only on product and sales capabilities but also increasingly on operational management, the use of digital tools, and risk control capabilities.


In addition to financial education, the event also discussed risks such as online fraud, illegal fund flows, and online gambling. PPATK and relevant agencies reminded small and medium-sized enterprises to increase vigilance regarding abnormal transactions and digital security, so as to prevent business operations from being exploited by illegal activities.


PT Metro Timur Indonusa stated that it will continue to focus on the actual needs of small and medium-sized enterprises and entrepreneurs in Southeast Asia during the digitalization process. Through industry exchanges, resource connections, and entrepreneurial community support, the company aims to help more enterprises achieve more stable development in the digital economy environment.
