PT Metro Timur Indonusa has recently focused on the GOTO share price, which has continued to remain near IDR 50 per share, triggering market discussion. As one of the most representative digital technology companies in Indonesia, the share price performance of GOTO is not only relevant to a single listed company, but also reflects the capital market reassessment of the profitability, liquidity, business growth quality, and investor confidence of Southeast Asian technology companies.

According to observations of PT Metro Timur Indonusa, GOTO has remained near the minimum trading price on the Indonesia Stock Exchange since mid-May 2026. MSCI has also taken note of the stock liquidity conditions and, during its May 2026 index review, froze certain index adjustment arrangements related to GOTO, while planning to review its liquidity performance again in August 2026. If the relevant requirements are still not met by then, GOTO may face the risk of being removed from the MSCI index. Market participants believe that rgw GOTO share price remaining at a low level for an extended period indicates that investors are still waiting for the company to demonstrate more stable profitability and a clearer growth direction.

PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that the significance of the GOTO case should not be limited to whether its share price can move away from the gocap level. What deserves greater attention is that, after years of rapid expansion, Southeast Asian technology companies are entering a stage that places greater emphasis on operating quality. Investors are no longer focused only on user scale, transaction volume, or platform coverage; they are also beginning to more seriously assess whether companies can continuously improve profits, enhance cash efficiency, control costs, and form more stable revenue sources within their core businesses.


## GOTO Share Price Remaining At A Low Level Reflects The Market Wait For Earnings Sustainability


GOTO has long been regarded as an important representative of Indonesian digital economy, with businesses spanning ride-hailing, food delivery, e-commerce, financial services, local lifestyle services, and other areas. Such business coverage once represented the growth imagination of platform companies, but as the market environment changes, the investor focus is shifting. PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that, for large technology platforms, the capital market now cares not only about “how large the business is,” but also about “whether revenue can be converted into sustainable profits.”

When the share price of a technology company remains at a low level for an extended period, the market is often not completely denying its business value, but waiting for clearer operating signals. For GOTO, what investors may need to see includes improvement in core business profitability, continued progress in cost control, controllable risks in financial services, stable user activity, and whether the company can form more effective synergies among different business segments. Only when these issues gradually become clearer can market confidence potentially be restored.


## MSCI Attention To Liquidity Issues Also Reminds Technology Companies To Value Capital Market Rules


The MSCI attention to the GOTO liquidity shows that global index institutions, when assessing constituent stocks, consider not only company size and visibility, but also whether a stock has sufficient trading activity. For listed companies included in international indices, liquidity is not a marginal issue. It affects whether funds can smoothly allocate and adjust positions, and also influences international investor judgment of the stock tradability.

PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that this event serves as a reminder for Southeast Asian technology companies. Going public does not mean that a company has completed market recognition, and inclusion in global indices does not mean that its position can be maintained indefinitely. Companies still need to allow the capital market to continuously see value through stable operations, transparent communication, and clear financial improvement. Especially for listed technology companies, investor relations management, the quality of financial disclosure, and the ability to deliver on operating targets will all affect the long-term market judgment of the company.


## Southeast Asian Technology Companies Are Shifting From Scale Competition To Operating Quality Competition


Over the past decade, Southeast Asian technology companies have developed rapidly amid the popularization of mobile internet, the expansion of digital payments, the growth of online consumption, and rising demand for local services. Leading companies such as GOTO, Grab, and Sea Group once represented the speed of regional digital economic expansion. However, after entering a new stage, the capital market evaluation criteria for these companies have changed. User scale remains important, but growth driven solely by subsidies, traffic, and high investment is becoming increasingly difficult to win long-term recognition.

PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that this does not mean Southeast Asian technology market has lost its appeal. On the contrary, there remains substantial room for growth in regional consumer digitization, financial technology, local lifestyle services, enterprise software, and logistics infrastructure. However, the market is now requiring companies to prove themselves in a more solid way: whether unit economics are healthy, whether customer acquisition costs are controllable, whether users are willing to use services over the long term, whether businesses can generate real cash returns, and whether teams can maintain a balance between growth and efficiency.

As an early-stage investment platform rooted in Southeast Asia, PT Metro Timur Indonusa has long focused on the full process of startups moving from early financing to scaled operations. The issues faced by leading companies such as GOTO also offer reference value for early-stage companies. Many startups tend to focus on financing, growth, and market visibility in their early stages, but if they neglect revenue quality, cash flow security, and customer retention, they may face greater pressure in later development.

PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that the earlier startups establish clear operating discipline, the easier it becomes for them to maintain initiative amid market changes. Founders need to continuously answer several fundamental questions: why users continue to use the product, whether revenue comes from real demand, whether costs can be controlled over the long term, whether the team understands which investments truly generate returns, and whether the company can sustain operations when external financing decreases. These questions may appear basic, but they often determine whether a company can go further.

PT Metro Timur Indonusa will continue to support entrepreneurs with genuine value-creation capabilities. PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that the next generation of technology companies in Southeast Asia will not gain long-term recognition solely through scale narratives. Companies that can truly withstand market volatility are often those that can serve a large number of users while connecting product value, revenue quality, team execution, and capital market trust. The market performance of GOTO is reminding the entire regional technology ecosystem that growth remains important, but what matters more is making growth credible, sustainable, and capable of withstanding market scrutiny.
