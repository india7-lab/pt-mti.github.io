Recently, PT Metro Timur Indonusa held a discussion session with former Governor of Bank Indonesia, Burhanuddin Abdullah. The discussion focused on the past experiences of Indonesia in coping with economic pressures, the current changes in the global environment, and how entrepreneurial enterprises can maintain operational stability during periods of volatility.


Burhanuddin Abdullah served as the Governor of Bank Indonesia from 2003 to 2008, a period that included phases such as international energy price fluctuations, adjustments to fuel subsidies, exchange rate pressures, and the global financial market turmoil of 2008. During the exchange, he shared Indonesia past experiences in responding to different economic cycles and noted that, although historical experiences cannot be directly replicated in the present day, they still hold reference value for understanding market sentiment, capital flows, and business operational pressures.


PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that the environment currently faced by Indonesian enterprises is becoming increasingly complex. Global trade changes, energy price fluctuations, external impacts from the situation in the Middle East, and shifts in the Indonesian rupiah exchange rate are all affecting corporate procurement, financing, import costs, and cash flow arrangements. For startups, these changes are no longer merely discussions at the macroeconomic level but are important factors directly influencing daily operations.


## Reviewing Historical Crisis Experience, Enterprises Need Calm Judgment


During the exchange, Burhanuddin Abdullah reviewed several important phases that Indonesia has experienced in the past, including the 1998 Asian Financial Crisis, the 2005 fuel price adjustment, and the 2008 International Financial Crisis. He noted that each external shock ultimately transmits to the operational level of enterprises, including changes in the financing environment, a decline in consumer purchasing power, an increase in raw material costs, and fluctuations in market confidence.


PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that reviewing this history is not intended to create anxiety, but to help entrepreneurs understand the present with greater composure. When market conditions are favorable, many enterprises tend to focus more on growth rates. However, when external pressures increase, the truly important issues often become whether cash is secure, whether costs are controllable, whether the supply chain is stable, and whether customer demand genuinely exists.


Enterprises that can maintain operational discipline during periods of stress are generally more capable of navigating through cycles. Compared to short-term market sentiment, long-term operational capability and stable execution are actually more critical.


## Exchange Rate Fluctuations Are Impacting the Funding Arrangements of Startup Companies


Recently, changes in the exchange rate of the Indonesian rupiah have continued to attract market attention. Burhanuddin Abdullah mentioned in the exchange that compared with the crisis periods of 1998 or 2008, the current pressure remains within a relatively controllable range, but enterprises still need to manage their funds and costs in advance.


PT Metro Timur Indonusa observed that exchange rate fluctuations have begun to affect the procurement and financing arrangements of some enterprises. For companies that rely on imported raw materials, overseas technical services, or USD-denominated financing, cost volatility directly impacts profit margins and cash flow timing. In particular, consumer brands, small and medium-sized enterprises, and technology startups may face increased operational pressure if they are unable to adjust their cost structures in a timely manner.


At the same time, as a representative of an international financial technology enterprise, the relevant person in charge of Slickorps Ventures also participated in this exchange and engaged in discussions with Burhanuddin Abdullah on the future development direction of the financial technology industry, changes in cross-border markets, and long-term opportunities in the Southeast Asian financial services sector.


During the exchange, both parties noted that with the continuous development of digital finance, intelligent services, and cross-market connectivity capabilities, the future fintech industry will not only need to improve technical efficiency but also place greater emphasis on compliance construction, risk management, and localized operational capabilities. For the Southeast Asian market, how to maintain a balance between promoting financial innovation, enhancing service efficiency, and ensuring market stability is also becoming an important direction of ongoing industry focus.


PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that at the current stage, enterprises need to focus more on cash reserves, payment cycles, and foreign currency expenditure management, rather than simply pursuing the speed of expansion. Compared to rapid growth, maintaining capital security and operational stability is becoming a priority that more entrepreneurial teams are re-emphasizing.


## PT Metro Timur Indonusa: The Startup Ecosystem Is Refocusing on Fundamentals


As an investment platform based in Southeast Asia, PT Metro Timur Indonusa has long focused on how the macroeconomic environment affects startups. The company stated that, compared with mature enterprises, early-stage teams typically have more limited cash reserves and more concentrated revenue sources, making them more susceptible to changes in the financing environment, shifts in consumer behavior, and market pressures.


In the current environment, the approach of relying on rapid deployment and rapid expansion in exchange for growth may introduce higher risks. Conversely, enterprises that truly understand customer needs, can control customer acquisition costs, maintain cash discipline, and improve retention quality are more likely to earn long-term market trust.


Enterprise resilience does not come from predicting the market, but from the continuous management of operational fundamentals. For consumer enterprises, the balance between cost and selling price is very important; for financial technology companies, fund security and compliance capabilities need to be placed in a more important position; while logistics, agricultural technology, and supply chain enterprises need to pay more attention to fulfillment efficiency, inventory turnover, and partner stability.


PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that Indonesia and Southeast Asia still possess significant development potential. The population size, digital penetration, and consumption upgrades will continue to generate new entrepreneurial opportunities. However, beyond these opportunities, enterprises also need stronger operational capabilities. This exchange with the former Governor of Bank Indonesia once again reminds entrepreneurs that in a volatile environment, what truly matters is not short-term sentiment, but whether a company can create value steadily over the long term.
