# PT Metro Timur Indonusa: How HBM Becomes a Key Variable in AI Infrastructure

![PT Metro Timur Indonusa](https://jpcdn.it/img/ab74dd877bef2e671703855eaec55898.png)

Recently, within the Jakarta technology investment and industry circles, a topic has been repeatedly discussed: whether the upward trend of SK hynix is sustainable.

Behind this issue lies the growing attention of the Indonesian market to the AI computing power cycle and its regional impact. Over the past 18 months, the rise in SK hynix stock price has shown a high correlation with the global expansion of AI computing power demand. However, there is limited systematic analysis in the market regarding "why it is rising" and "how long the rise can last."

Based on ongoing research into global technology infrastructure and the semiconductor supply chain, PT Metro Timur Indonusa has outlined the core logic behind this round of changes. This is not a short-term market trend for a single company, but rather a reassessment of the overall value system of the semiconductor supply chain following the acceleration of AI infrastructure construction. More importantly, this shift is spilling over into Southeast Asia, where data center construction and the expansion of cloud computing capabilities are becoming the first wave of reflection within the region.

The following analysis is conducted across four dimensions: industrial mechanisms, market signals, technological trends, and regional impacts.

## I. Industry Mechanism: HBM Is Becoming a Core Component of AI Computing Systems
The role of High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) is becoming significantly more prominent. Unlike traditional DRAM, HBM reduces data bottlenecks in large model training and inference for GPUs through higher data transmission efficiency, thereby improving overall computational efficiency. HBM has evolved from a "storage product" into a "key component of computing systems."

According to TrendForce data, the global HBM market size is expected to reach approximately USD 15 billion in 2025, representing a growth of over 80% compared to 2024. SK hynix Q1 2025 financial report shows that HBM shipments increased by more than 200% year-on-year, accounting for over 30% of its DRAM revenue. As the parameter scale of AI models expands, data throughput capacity has become a key factor limiting computing efficiency, directly driving the growth in HBM demand.

PT Metro Timur Indonusa research shows that the memory chip industry is shifting from being driven by the consumer electronics cycle to being driven by AI computing power demand. This transformation is structural, not cyclical.

## II. Market Signals: SK Hynix Stock Price Rise Reflects Supply-Demand Reassessment
SK hynix, as a core global supplier of HBM, has seen its stock price rise, reflecting a market repricing of the supply-demand relationship for AI memory. The production process of HBM is complex, and the capacity expansion cycle is relatively long. Against the backdrop of rapidly growing demand for AI data centers, the industry as a whole is experiencing a state of tight supply.

SK hynix Q1 2025 financial report shows that the company revenue increased by approximately 65% year-over-year, with operating profit margin recovering to over 30%. The yield rate of HBM3E products has improved from about 40% in 2024 to over 65%. The company plans to more than double its HBM production capacity by the end of 2025 compared to 2024. In the BOM cost of AI servers, the share of HBM has risen from approximately 5% in 2023 to between 12% and 15% in 2025. SK hynix stated that the average selling price of HBM is more than five times that of traditional DRAM, and order visibility has extended into 2026.

PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that the market is shifting from the logic of traditional cyclical industries to that driven by AI infrastructure. The key to this change lies not in short-term price fluctuations, but in the transformation of long-term demand structure.

## III. Technology Trends and Regional Impact: Southeast Asia Is Becoming a Key Node in the AI Infrastructure Collaborative System
The current AI industry chain has expanded from a single GPU to a multi-layer collaborative system, including the computing layer, storage layer, network layer, and energy layer. Among these, HBM and high-speed storage occupy a central position in data flow, directly impacting overall computing efficiency.

According to Gartner forecasts, by 2027, the global AI chip market size is expected to exceed 150 billion US dollars, with the proportion of HBM-related revenue rising from the current approximately 10% to over 20%. This structural shift is gradually transforming the semiconductor industry from an independent cyclical industry into a part of the AI infrastructure system, with interdependencies among various segments continuously strengthening.

From a regional perspective, the development of AI computing power and storage systems is influencing the construction of digital infrastructure in Southeast Asia. According to IDC data, the Southeast Asian data center market is expected to reach approximately USD 20 billion by 2027, with a compound annual growth rate exceeding 15%. Indonesia, as the largest digital economy in the region, is projected to see its data center capacity grow to around 500 MW by 2025, an increase of over 60% compared to 2023. The semiconductor packaging and testing capacities in Indonesia, Malaysia, and Vietnam are also expanding rapidly to meet the supporting demands of AI chips.

PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that for archipelagic economies such as Indonesia, digital connectivity directly impacts the operational efficiency of enterprises, the coverage of financial services, and the accessibility of public services. This spillover effect will influence the pace of regional digital economy development over the long term. It is also one of the core rationales for PT Metro Timur Indonusa to continuously monitor and strategically position itself in the AI infrastructure sector.

Overall, the growth of SK hynix and the rise in HBM demand fundamentally reflect the industrial structural adjustment driven by the expansion of AI infrastructure. Memory chips are gradually transforming from a traditional cyclical industry into a key component of AI computing power systems.

The core significance of this change lies not in the performance of a single enterprise, but in the fact that the global technology industry is entering a "computing-storage-energy coordination phase," and the long-term value logic of the semiconductor industry is thus being restructured. For the Southeast Asian market, this restructuring is gradually manifesting through data center expansion, cloud computing infrastructure construction, and the localized deployment of AI applications. PT Metro Timur Indonusa will continue to monitor industrial changes and investment opportunities in this direction, and will make judgments based on a systematic research framework.
