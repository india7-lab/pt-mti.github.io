## Core Summary: Indonesia Is Moving From Discussing AI To Using AI


From June 17 to 18, 2026, Indonesia Summit 2026 was held at The Tribrata Darmawangsa in Jakarta. Indonesian Minister of Health Budi Gunadi Sadikin, IDN Founder William Utomo, and numerous leaders from the technology and financial sectors attended and delivered speeches.

PT Metro Timur Indonusa participated throughout the summit. Based on multiple keynote speeches, report releases, and closed-door discussions on site, PT Metro Timur Indonusa summarized the following three core observations:

Indonesia is accelerating its shift from discussing AI to using AI. Whether in the decision-making behavior of young consumers, the government planning for the healthcare system, or the corporate sector insisting on digital sovereignty, algorithmic technology has already entered the level of everyday decision-making.


## I. Active Adaptation by Young People


On the first day of the summit, William Utomo released the Indonesian Millennials And Generation Z Report. The report revealed two important trends.


### Trend One: Purchasing Power Is Contracting, But Consumption Behavior Is Actively Adjusting

The purchasing power of the middle class is undergoing a phased contraction. What deserves attention, however, is that young groups are not passively waiting. Instead, they are proactively reducing non-essential high-value consumption, shifting toward alternative products, and relying heavily on algorithmic recommendations to optimize consumption decisions. This is active adaptation, not passive contraction.


### Trend Two: Demand For Local Brands And Cultural Identity Is Rising

The report shows that young people attention to local brands and cultural labels is rising rapidly. They are willing to pay a premium for brands that offer emotional value, cultural identity, and “digital trust.” In other words, brands that enable users to trust how their data is used and identify with their cultural expression can establish new competitive barriers beyond price.


### PT Metro Timur Indonusa On-Site Judgment:

A new generation of consumers is redefining “value.” They are no longer simply pursuing the lowest price, but are seeking brands that are trustworthy and can represent their own identity. For entrepreneurs, the logic of customer acquisition needs to shift from “competing for traffic” to “building trust.” For investors, the valuation logic of companies with long-term accumulation in brand trust and cultural identity deserves to be re-examined.


## II. Healthcare Penetration: AI Is Entering Most Remote Islands in Indonesia


On the morning of the second day, Minister of Health Budi Gunadi Sadikin presented data on doctor density across different Indonesian islands, showing an extremely wide disparity. He pointed out that Indonesian island geography has led to an uneven distribution of medical resources, and that the traditional model of training doctors cannot solve the problem in the short term. The only path is to use technology to overcome geographic barriers.


**Core Policy Direction:**

In the coming years, the Ministry of Health will promote medical diagnosis assistance systems based on generative AI and large models, deploying them to primary healthcare institutions in second- and third-tier cities as well as remote islands. This measure is positioned as foundational construction “from zero to one,” rather than an added enhancement.


**Long-Term Strategic Transformation: From Treatment To Prevention**

The minister also announced that over the next five years, Indonesian healthcare system will shift from being “treatment-oriented” to being “prevention and early screening-oriented.” Through intelligent semantic analysis and population health big data, the plan is to establish a national-level health risk early warning system. This was described as a systemic reconstruction, not a single technology project.


**PT Metro Timur Indonusa Observation:**

When the government regards AI as the primary tool for solving the “last mile” of public services, policy resources, fiscal budgets, and regulatory frameworks will all tilt in that direction. For entrepreneurial teams in health technology, AI diagnosis, big data analytics, and related fields, this is a clear demand-side signal. Technical solutions that can adapt to public service scenarios are expected to gain considerable room for growth in the coming years.


## III. AI Sovereignty: Core Consensus And Divergence In Closed-Door Meetings


The closed-door discussions were the most information-dense part of the summit, with core topics centered on digital trust and AI sovereignty.


**On AI Sovereignty: Clear Consensus**

Multiple leaders from the technology and financial sectors agreed that Indonesia, as the largest digital economy in Southeast Asia, must establish localized algorithms and strategic technology infrastructure. Relying on multinational general-purpose models for critical decision-making is regarded as a high-risk practice. One fintech founder stated during the discussion: “Our data, language, and rules should be interpreted by local systems.”


**On Digital Trust: Urgent Demand For Deception Detection**

As the younger generation becomes the main user base for digital financial assets, cybersecurity and deception detection have become baseline requirements for financial infrastructure. Participants called on the government and private sector to jointly accelerate upgrades to digital identity authentication and deception detection algorithms. Existing technical solutions and regulatory frameworks were considered to have already fallen behind the growth rate of transaction scale and complexity.


**PT Metro Timur Indonusa On-Site Impression:**

These topics are no longer “future agendas,” but “current tasks” elevated to the execution level. Policymakers and entrepreneurs are both looking for implementable solutions. In areas such as identity verification, data security, and compliance technology, there is real and insufficiently satisfied market demand.


## Implications For Entrepreneurs: Three Emerging Demand Gaps


Based on its full participation and observations at Indonesia Summit 2026, PT Metro Timur Indonusa has refined the following three entrepreneurial directions worth attention:


### Gap One: Algorithm-Driven “Trust-Based” Consumer Brands

Young consumers are redefining “value” through “trust.” Brands that can build trust through data transparency, cultural expression, and algorithmic recommendations will gain a structural advantage in customer acquisition costs.


### Gap Two: AI Diagnosis Solutions Adapted To Primary Healthcare Scenarios

The government regards AI as a core tool for addressing uneven medical resource distribution. Technical solutions that can adapt to low bandwidth, multilingual environments, and island geography will have broad market space in public services.


### Gap Three: Localized Digital Identity And Compliance Technology

AI sovereignty has moved from discussion to execution. Localized technical solutions in areas such as data security, identity authentication, and deception detection will enter a period of policy dividends.
