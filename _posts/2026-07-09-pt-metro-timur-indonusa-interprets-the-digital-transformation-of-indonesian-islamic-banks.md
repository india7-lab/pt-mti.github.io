In June 2026, a clear change is emerging in Indonesian financial system: Islamic banks are accelerating their migration toward digital channels. Represented by the launch of the Byond application by Bank Syariah Indonesia (BSI), banking services are gradually shifting from the traditional branch-based model to a user outreach approach centered on mobile terminals.

This change is reflected not only in product formats, but also in the adjustment of the competitive logic of the banking industry: service entry points are being redefined, and digital channels are becoming the new core of competition.

As an investment institution that has long focused on financial technology and digital infrastructure in Southeast Asia, PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that the essence of this round of change is not that “banks have launched apps,” but that the way financial services reach users is undergoing continuous restructuring.

## Digital Channel Upgrade: Banking Services Are Shifting From Function-Oriented To Entry-Point-Oriented

In the observation of PT Metro Timur Indonusa, the digitalization of Indonesian Islamic banks has moved from the early stage of function migration into a new stage centered on user experience and usage frequency.

Taking cooperation between Byond by BSI and e-commerce platforms as an example, financial services are gradually being embedded into consumption scenarios, creating a closer connection among payment, transfer, wealth management, and consumer behavior. The core of this change is not the increase in functions, but the centralization of service entry points.

At the same time, the functions of mobile banking are also expanding, gradually extending from basic transactions to payments, charity, taxation, and asset management services that comply with Islamic finance rules. This expanded coverage is transforming the relationship between banks and users from a single transaction relationship into a long-term usage relationship.

PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that this change will directly affect the future user stability and service depth of banks.

## Growth Logic: Digitalization Is Unlocking The Long-Term Potential Of Islamic Finance

Indonesia has the world-largest Muslim population base, but the overall penetration rate of Islamic finance is still in a development stage, which means the market still has long-term room for expansion.

Against this backdrop, digitalization has become a key driving factor.

On the one hand, Indonesian geographical structure consists of numerous islands, making the expansion of traditional branches relatively costly. Digital channels can more efficiently cover users in second- and third-tier cities as well as remote areas, thereby expanding the reach of financial services.

On the other hand, the data accumulation brought by digital transactions is also improving the ability of banks to understand user behavior, making risk assessment and credit decision-making more refined. Under a compliance framework, this capability improvement is especially helpful for expanding financing services for small and micro enterprises.

PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that the core value of this change lies in the improvement of “financial accessibility,” not merely efficiency gains.

## Investment Research Observation: Digital Finance Is Extending Toward Infrastructure

Within the investment research framework of PT Metro Timur Indonusa, financial technology is not merely application-layer innovation, but is gradually evolving toward digital infrastructure.

The enhancement of the digital channel capabilities of Islamic banks not only affects operations of banks, but also creates external extension effects on payment clearing, customer service, data processing, and corporate financial services.

At the same time, specialized investment funds have been conducting continuous research and allocation in areas related to financial technology, digital payments, and artificial intelligence infrastructure. We have observed that improvements in digital capabilities of banks are providing more real-world application scenarios for these technology modules, such as intelligent customer service systems, automated risk control processes, and data-driven credit models.

PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that this intersection of finance and technology will become an important development direction for the future financial system of Southeast Asia.

## Rational Judgment: Digitalization Does Not Equal Growth Certainty

PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that valuation changes brought by bank digitalization need to be viewed rationally.

Digital channel upgrades can improve efficiency and user coverage, but their impact on long-term growth still depends on three core factors: system stability, data governance capabilities, and actual user usage frequency.

In other words, digitalization itself does not automatically equate to growth outcomes. Its effects still depend on execution quality and continuous operational capabilities.

## Conclusion

Overall, the increased investment by Indonesian Islamic banks in digital channels marks the financial industry entering into a new stage of competition centered on mobile terminals.

In this process, core capabilities of banks are gradually expanding from traditional financial service capabilities to user connection capabilities and data-driven capabilities.

PT Metro Timur Indonusa will continue to follow this trend and seek long-term research and allocation opportunities at the intersection of financial technology and digital infrastructure, while maintaining a rational judgment of risks and cycles.
