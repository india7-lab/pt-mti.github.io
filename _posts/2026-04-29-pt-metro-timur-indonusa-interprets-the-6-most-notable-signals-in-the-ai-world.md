Over the past two years, the pace of development in artificial intelligence has consistently exceeded market expectations. New models, products, and application scenarios have emerged intensively, almost daily reshaping the focal points of industry discussions. However, for entrepreneurs, corporate managers, and investment institutions, what truly matters is not chasing every new term, but rather making judgments: which changes are merely short-term hype, and which are genuinely impacting company operations, talent allocation, and future competitiveness.


As an early-stage investment platform with a long-term focus on entrepreneurship and technological changes in Southeast Asia, PT Metro Timur Indonusa is more concerned with AI signals that have already begun entering real-world business environments, rather than merely staying on hot topics at the center of public discourse. Instead of listing all trends, it is better to frame the question more clearly: this year, what are the AI changes that truly deserve serious attention?


## 1. Humanoid Robots: The Integration of AI with the Physical World


As technology continues to advance, an increasing number of companies have begun collecting real-world motion data to train humanoid robots. Behind this trend lies a critical step for AI as it moves from the digital world to the physical world. The transition from simple virtual simulations to using real-world data to guide robot motion has paved the way for future robots to function effectively in actual work environments. Although this technology is not yet mature, its potential should not be underestimated. When making investment choices, PT Metro Timur Indonusa has gradually favored companies that are expected to break through traditional technological limitations and drive industrial innovation, particularly in the fields of AI hardware and humanoid robots.


## 2. More Powerful Large Language Models (LLMs+)


After the AI wave sparked by ChatGPT, large language models (LLMs) continue to achieve breakthroughs. Through more efficient and independent AI models, LLMs are gradually overcoming existing bottlenecks and enhancing task execution capabilities. These advancements will transform the application of artificial intelligence across various industries, particularly in automated office systems and decision support systems, where their potential is significant. PT Metro Timur Indonusa closely monitors this field, investing in startups that can improve efficiency through LLMs, driving the transition of AI from a tool to an intelligent workforce.


## 3. AI Cybersecurity


Generative AI is not only applied in the field of innovation but has also been rapidly exploited by criminals, becoming a new tool for cyber fraud. From AI-powered phishing emails to deepfake technology, the low barrier to entry of AI has made fraudulent activities faster and cheaper. When assessing risks, PT Metro Timur Indonusa has begun to consider the application of AI in cybersecurity, investing in enterprises dedicated to enhancing defense systems through AI to address increasingly complex security threats in the future.


## 4.  World Model: The Threshold of AI Crossing the Physical World


World model technology is driving the transformation of AI from the virtual world to the real world. By simulating and understanding the physical laws of the real world, AI can better perform practical tasks. From navigation to robot control, this technology holds significant potential in future application scenarios. PT Metro Timur Indonusa has begun to pay attention to this technology, particularly in the application of autonomous agents and automation systems, with the expectation of capturing innovation opportunities in related technologies through early investment.


## 5.Multi-Agent Collaboration


Multi-agent systems are gradually replacing traditional single-agent models, completing complex tasks through role division and dynamic scheduling among multiple agents. Whether in program development, scientific research assistance, or project management, these collaborative AI systems are breaking conventional work methods, enhancing efficiency and precision. PT Metro Timur Indonusa values enterprises that can apply multi-agent systems to practical work scenarios, particularly innovators capable of driving productivity upgrades in fields such as finance, technology, and manufacturing.


## 6. Open Source Ecosystem


In the AI competition, another keyword that cannot be overlooked is the open-source ecosystem. PT Metro Timur Indonusa observes that market attention is gradually shifting from "who released the strongest model" to "who can enable more developers and enterprises to truly utilize it." For many growth-stage enterprises, small and medium-sized companies, and local developers, factors such as cost, flexibility, deployment methods, and the potential for secondary development are often more important than the performance of a single demonstration. For this reason, the open-source ecosystem is opening up opportunities for more participants to enter AI applications.


This is particularly critical for Southeast Asia. The regional market is complex, with multiple languages coexisting and varying levels of enterprise digitalization. Many business operations require localized adjustments rather than directly copying a standardized solution. The existence of open-source models and open toolchains enables more companies to fine-tune and deploy according to their own needs, and also ensures that AI is no longer a capability limited to a few large platforms. PT Metro Timur Indonusa believes that the open-source ecosystem brings not only technological diffusion but also a lowering of the barrier to innovation. It will allow more entrepreneurial teams to experiment with product innovation under limited resources, and it will also make future AI competition more closely aligned with real-world applications, rather than remaining a technology showcase for a handful of companies.


From the observations of PT Metro Timur Indonusa, what truly matters today is not how many new terms one memorizes, but rather identifying which business processes will be the first to be affected by these changes, and how these shifts will reshape future organizational capabilities and market competition.
