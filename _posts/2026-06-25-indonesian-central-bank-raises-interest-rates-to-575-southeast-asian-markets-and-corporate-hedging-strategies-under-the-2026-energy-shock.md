## Core Summary: The “Scissors Gap” Among Global Central Banks And Southeast Asian Challenges


In June 2026, although the FIFA World Cup hosted by the United States, Canada, and Mexico is in full swing, the global macroeconomic contest is reshaping the underlying logic of Southeast Asian markets. Energy supply shocks triggered by geopolitical conflicts have led to a sharp divergence in global central bank policies.


For Southeast Asian countries that are highly dependent on energy imports, such as Indonesia and the Philippines, defending local currency exchange rates has become the top priority. MetroTimur-X Academy, the macro research institution under PT Metro Timur Indonusa, points out: interest rate hikes are not an option, but a survival threshold. If enterprises fail to understand the transmission mechanism of this round of imported inflation, their profit margins will be rapidly eroded.


## I. June 2026 Developments: The Logic Behind Indonesia And The “Emergency Interest Rate Hikes” in Philippines


1. Bank Indonesia (BI): Two Consecutive Hikes To Defend The Rupiah


Latest Action: On June 18, Bank Indonesia (BI) announced that it would raise the benchmark interest rate by 25 basis points to 5.75%.


Urgency: This is the second interest rate hike within this month, following an emergency adjustment already made on June 9.


Core Logic: The core objective is to increase the interest rate spread, attract foreign capital to remain in the country, ease pressure from the current account deficit caused by energy imports, and prevent the rupiah from further sliding toward the key psychological threshold of IDR 16,000 per USD 1.


2. Japanese “Historic Turnaround” And Europe And The Wait-And-See Approach by The United States 


Bank Of Japan: On June 16, it raised interest rates to 1.0%, ending 31 years of ultra-loose monetary policy and marking the complete end of the global negative interest rate era.


Federal Reserve And European Central Bank: The European Central Bank restarted interest rate hikes on June 11 to combat the energy dilemma, while the Federal Reserve chose to “stay put” due to fluctuating domestic consumption data.


**Knowledge Point: What Is Imported Inflation?**


When international oil prices/LNG prices rise and the local currency, the rupiah, depreciates, the cost of imported production inputs, such as chemical products and energy, surges. This is no longer a simple price increase, but a core cost variable that directly erodes the gross margins of manufacturing enterprises.


## II. In-Depth Analysis: Why Do Countries React Differently To The Same “Energy Shock”?


MetroTimur-X Academy believes that the essence of this round of policy divergence lies in differences in each country “energy self-sufficiency rate”:


### Category One: Economies Highly Dependent On Energy Imports


Representative countries include the eurozone, Japan, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The policy tendency of these countries is emergency interest rate hikes or continued interest rate hikes. The underlying logic is that once the local currency depreciates, it will immediately translate into higher import costs. Therefore, high interest rates must be used to maintain exchange rate stability and prevent imported inflation from spiraling out of control.


### Category Two: Economies With Relative Energy Autonomy


Representative countries include the United States, Canada, and Australia. The policy tendency of these countries is to stay put or take a wait-and-see approach. They possess abundant shale oil, natural gas, or mineral resources and therefore have stronger anti-inflation capabilities. As a result, their central banks have a longer window for observing data and do not need to rush to follow with interest rate hikes.


**Key Conclusion:** As long as geopolitical conflicts do not end and energy prices remain high, the interest rate center in Southeast Asia cannot decline. This means that “cheap money” will completely disappear in the second half of 2026.


## III. Practical Recommendations: How Can Southeast Asian Entrepreneurs Navigate The Interest Rate Hike Cycle?


Based on the investment research experience by PT Metro Timur Indonusa, we have refined the following three cash flow protection strategies for entrepreneurs:


### Strategy One: Restructure Debt And Avoid The “Floating Rate” Trap


Risk Point: As BI raises interest rates, the cost of floating-rate loans offered by local banks, such as KPR/KUR commercial loans, rises sharply.


Recommended Action: Prioritize fixed-rate financing. Review the balance sheet, reduce the proportion of debt denominated in USD or with foreign exchange exposure, and avoid currency mismatch risks.


### Strategy Two: Elevate “Exchange Rate Hedging” From The Finance Office To The Boardroom


Harsh Reality: The volatility of the rupiah in June is no longer “financial noise,” but a core strategic risk.


Recommended Action: For enterprises involving imported raw materials, such as wheat, plastic pellets, and electronic components, immediately activate forward foreign exchange settlement and sales or foreign exchange options. Lock in the core procurement exchange rate for the next six months, turn uncertainty into fixed costs, and thereby protect gross margins from erosion.


## Strategy Three: Pursue “Organic Growth” Rather Than A Traffic Bubble


Macroeconomic Perspective: When global liquidity tightens, capital will flow back to the United States, and the model of relying on venture capital to burn money in exchange for traffic will become unsustainable.


Recommended Action: Return to local insights. During an interest rate hike cycle, enterprises that survive often focus on genuine local necessities, such as digital transformation and cost-reduction and efficiency-improvement SaaS, while integrating social value into supply chain resilience.


## Appendix: Macroeconomic FAQ For Entrepreneurs


Q1: Will Bank Indonesia Continue To Raise Interest Rates?

Most likely, yes. As long as the rupiah exchange rate faces the risk of breaking key levels, such as falling below the 16,200 threshold, BI will not hesitate and will continue to adopt defensive interest rate hike measures.


Q2: For Small Business Owners, What Is The Simplest Hedging Method?

Shorten the inventory cycle. Reduce stockpiling of raw materials and adopt a “small orders with quick replenishment” model to avoid tying up large amounts of working capital in a high-interest-rate environment.


Q3: Is Now Still A Good Time For Financing?

Yes, but the standards have changed. Projects with positive cash flow will be highly favored. The era of financing purely based on concepts is over. Capital is now looking for “patient capital” and “resilient business models.”
